How this test works

No magic, no horoscopes. Your ranked values run through a short, transparent pipeline grounded in decades of personality and values research. Here's the whole thing, start to finish.

{# --- D10: methodology deep-dive --- #}

  1. 1 · Start with values

    The 100 values come from Acceptance and Commitment Therapy, where a value is a freely chosen direction for living, not a goal to tick off. You rank the ones that matter most.

  2. 2 · Weight by rank

    Earlier picks count for more. Your #1 carries the most weight and the last the least, so the result leans on what you care about most without ignoring the rest.

  3. 3 · Score against average

    Each value maps onto the ten Schwartz basic values. Your scores are compared to what an average selection would produce, so the reading reflects what's distinctive about your choices rather than what's simply common.

  4. 4 · Sharpen the picture

    The profile is gently exaggerated so real peaks and valleys stand out, turning a flat average into a recognisable shape.

  5. 5 · Match to the nearest type

    Your profile is compared to each of the ten archetypes' signatures, and the closest match becomes your type. A close call between two becomes a blend.

  6. 6 · Translate to the Big Five

    The same profile is expressed in Big Five (OCEAN) traits, so your result reads in familiar personality language.

{# --- D11: glossary --- #}

Glossary

Value
A chosen direction for how you want to live and act (compassion, curiosity, courage), not a goal you complete.
Schwartz circumplex
Ten basic human values arranged in a circle, where neighbours are compatible and opposite values conflict.
Emphasis
How strongly a motivation shows up in your choices compared to an average selection. 1.0× is exactly average.
Big Five
The five broad personality traits (Openness, Conscientiousness, Extraversion, Agreeableness, Emotional Stability), the most validated model in personality science.
Archetype
One of ten personality types, each defined by a distinct signature of motivations.
